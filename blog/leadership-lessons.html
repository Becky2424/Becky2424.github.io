What Managing 13 Locations Taught Me About Leadership

February 15, 2026

When I started in the self-storage industry, I managed a single facility. Today, I serve as a training resource and operational partner across a 13-location district. The path between those two points taught me more about leadership than any textbook ever could.

People First, Always

The most important lesson I have learned is that operations run on people, not processes. Processes matter, but they only work when the people behind them feel supported, heard, and empowered to do their best work.

Early in my career, I focused heavily on systems and procedures. Over time, I realized that investing in my team's development yielded far greater returns than perfecting a checklist.

Consistency Builds Trust

Managing multiple locations means you cannot be everywhere at once. What you can do is set clear expectations and hold yourself to the same standards you set for your team. When people know what to expect from you, they are more willing to meet those expectations themselves.

Leadership is not about being in charge. It is about taking care of those in your charge.

Embrace the Learning Curve

Every new facility, every new team member, every new challenge is an opportunity to learn something. I have managed locations during launches, transitions, and turnarounds. Each situation required a different approach, and being willing to adapt has been one of my greatest strengths.

Looking Forward

As I continue to grow in my career and explore new areas like AI and technology, I carry these leadership principles with me. The tools may change, but the fundamentals of good leadership remain the same: listen, support, set the standard, and never stop learning.
